About Me

My name is Wenxiang Ying (in Chinese: 应文祥). I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Chemistry at the University of Pennsylvania, working under the supervision of Prof. Abraham Nitzan. My research focuses on developing theoretical description of light matter interaction processes in confined environments.

I received my M.S. and Ph.D. in Chemistry from the University of Rochester (advisor: Prof. Pengfei Huo) in 2025, with a dissertation titled Theory and Quantum Dynamics Investigations of Polariton Chemistry, Photo-Physics, and Transport. Before that, I obtained my B.S. in Chemical Physics from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in 2020. My undergraduate research was conducted within the Quantum Statistical Dynamics Laboratory and supervised by Prof. Xiao Zheng, Prof. Yao Wang, and Prof. YiJing Yan. I visited the Boston University during the summer of 2019 as a research intern under the supervision of Prof. David F. Coker. Since then I have been passionate about theoretical and computational chemistry, particularly quantum dynamics.

Whether you are an established scholar looking for collaboration or a fresh scientist figuring how to start, I’d be happy to hear from you!
