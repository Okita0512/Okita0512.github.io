About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Chemistry at the University of Pennsylvania, working with Prof. Abraham Nitzan. My research focuses on quantum dynamics and cavity quantum electrodynamics, with an emphasis on how confined light–matter interactions reshape chemical reactivity, photophysics, and transport.

I received my M.S. and Ph.D. in Chemistry from the University of Rochester (advisor: Prof. Pengfei Huo) in 2025. My doctoral work developed theoretical frameworks and quantum dynamics simulations for polaritonic chemistry, photophysics, and transport. Before that, I obtained my B.S. in Chemical Physics from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC).

Research Interests

  • Quantum Dynamics Methods: hierarchical equations of motion, approximate quantum master equations, tensor-network approaches, mixed quantum–classical dynamics, and ring-polymer molecular dynamics.
  • Cavity Quantum Electrodynamics & Polaritons: exciton-polariton photophysics and transport, vibrational polariton chemistry, collective strong light–matter coupling, and cavity spectroscopy.
  • Finite-Difference Time-Domain (FDTD) Simulations of nanoparticle metasurfaces and nanophotonic structures.
  • Ab Initio Electronic Structure & Nonadiabatic Dynamics: excited-state methods (CASSCF, CASPT2, MRCI, EOM-CC, TDDFT) and ab initio nonadiabatic molecular dynamics for photoexcited systems.
